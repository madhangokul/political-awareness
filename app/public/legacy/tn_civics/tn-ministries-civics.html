Tamil Nadu · State Government
Ministry Portfolios — Civics Cheat Sheet

What does the state actually govern?

A state government is divided into ministries — each one responsible for a specific slice of public life. Think of each ministry as a department that exists because something needs to be run, regulated, or protected. This is your map to understanding what they are, why they exist, and what happens because of them.

🏛 Governance — How the state runs itself
👑
General Administration
"The nervous system of government"
Why it exists
Every government department needs coordination, staffing, and policy oversight. GA is the hub that keeps all other ministries talking to each other.
What it controls
IAS officers — posting, transfer, promotion
Cabinet decisions and government orders
District Collectors and revenue officers
Chief Minister's Office coordination
In practice: When a government scheme is launched, GA decides which officer goes where to implement it.
💰
Finance
"Where every rupee comes from and goes"
Why it exists
The state earns money (taxes, grants) and must spend it wisely. Finance ensures every department gets funded, and spending is accounted for.
What it controls
State Budget — presented every year
Treasury — all government payments
Pension and retirement benefits of employees
Loans and borrowings by the state
In practice: Before any department spends money on a new scheme, Finance Ministry clears the allocation.
🚔
Home (Police & Public Order)
"Keeping the peace — literally"
Why it exists
Law & order is the first duty of any government. Home Ministry manages the entire police apparatus and ensures internal security.
What it controls
Tamil Nadu Police — all postings, operations
IPS officer appointments
Prisons and correctional facilities
Fire & Rescue Services
In practice: A protest in Chennai? The police deployment, permissions, and management — all flow from this ministry.
📋
Revenue & Disaster Management
"Land records + crisis response"
Why it exists
Land is the most contested resource in any state. Revenue ministry maintains ownership records and steps in when disasters strike.
What it controls
Patta (land ownership) records and disputes
District administration via Collectors
State Disaster Response Force (SDRF)
Flood and cyclone relief operations
In practice: Cyclone Michaung hits Chennai — Revenue Ministry coordinates evacuation, relief camps, and compensation.
🧾
Commercial Taxes
"The state's biggest income source"
Why it exists
Businesses and trade generate taxes. This ministry collects GST (state share), stamp duties, and ensures businesses don't evade tax.
What it controls
GST collection — State's share from Centre
VAT on petroleum products
Property registration & stamp duty
Tax enforcement and audits
In practice: When you register a house, the registration fee (stamp duty) flows into the state treasury via this ministry.
⛏️
Mines & Natural Resources
"Who controls what's under the ground"
Why it exists
Minerals and natural resources belong to the state. This ministry controls who mines what, prevents illegal quarrying, and earns royalties.
What it controls
Sand, granite, and quarry leases
Mining licenses and royalty collection
Prevention of illegal sand mining
In practice: The sand you use for construction — someone paid the state government a royalty. This ministry collects it.
🏗 Infrastructure — Building the physical state
🛣️
Public Works (PWD)
"The government's construction company"
Why it exists
Roads, bridges, government buildings — someone has to plan, build, and maintain them. That's PWD. Every piece of public infrastructure is their responsibility.
What it controls
State highways and district roads
Government buildings, courts, offices
Bridges and flyovers
Minor ports along the coast
In practice: The road in front of your house that needs repair — that work order goes through PWD.
🏙️
Municipal Administration
"Running every town and city"
Why it exists
Cities need local governing bodies — corporations, municipalities. This ministry oversees all urban local bodies and ensures city services function.
What it controls
15 Municipal Corporations incl. Chennai
Urban water supply and sewage
Street lights, waste collection
Building plan approvals
In practice: Your Chennai Corporation property tax? Goes to a body under this ministry's oversight.
🏘️
Rural Development & Panchayats
"Governance at the village level"
Why it exists
Most of Tamil Nadu lives in villages. Panchayat Raj gives villages self-governance. This ministry funds and administers all 12,600+ village panchayats.
What it controls
Village panchayats and union panchayats
MGNREGS (rural employment scheme)
Rural roads, drains, sanitation
Panchayat-level construction works
In practice: The cement road inside your village, the community hall — funded and monitored by this ministry.
🏠
Housing & Town Planning
"A home for every family"
Why it exists
Housing is a basic need. Slum clearance, affordable housing schemes, and planned urban growth are impossible without a dedicated ministry.
What it controls
Tamil Nadu Housing Board (TNHB)
Slum Clearance Board — rehab schemes
CMDA — Chennai's master planning body
Rural housing under PMAY
In practice: The "2BHK for poor" scheme in TN — executed through this ministry's agencies.
🚌
Transport
"Moving people across the state"
Why it exists
Public transport is the backbone of daily life. The state runs bus corporations connecting every district — private vehicles alone can't serve everyone.
What it controls
TNSTC, MTC — state bus corporations
Motor Vehicle Act enforcement
Driving licenses and vehicle registration
Chennai Metro oversight (state share)
In practice: The free bus ride scheme for women in TN — a policy of this ministry, funded by Finance.
⚡
Electricity & Energy
"Power to every home"
Why it exists
Electricity is infrastructure. The state owns power generation plants and distribution networks. Someone must regulate tariffs and ensure supply.
What it controls
TANGEDCO — state electricity utility
Power tariffs and subsidy decisions
Solar and wind energy policy
Rural electrification programs
In practice: Free electricity units for domestic consumers — this ministry sets the slab, Finance funds the subsidy.
💧
Water Resources (Irrigation)
"Managing every drop of water"
Why it exists
Agriculture and drinking water both depend on rivers, tanks, and canals. The state must manage and distribute water resources equitably — especially in a drought-prone state.
What it controls
Major dams — Mettur, Vaigai, Bhavanisagar
Canal systems for agricultural water
Groundwater regulation
Cauvery water dispute management
In practice: The annual Mettur dam opening for Cauvery delta farmers — an order from this ministry.
📚 People's Basics — Education, Health & Food
🏫
School Education
"From age 5 to Class 12"
Why it exists
Nearly 44,000 government schools serve millions of children. Without state-run schooling, education would be inaccessible to the poor. Someone must hire teachers, write curricula, and run exams.
What it controls
All government and government-aided schools
Teacher recruitment and transfers
Tamil Nadu Board syllabus and exams
Noon meal scheme, free textbooks, uniforms
In practice: Free breakfast in government schools? That's the CM's Breakfast Scheme — a policy of this ministry.
🎓
Higher Education
"Universities, colleges and research"
Why it exists
Tamil Nadu has 500+ arts & science colleges and 500+ engineering colleges. The state needs to regulate quality, set admission rules, and fund public universities.
What it controls
State universities — Anna, Madras, Bharathiar
Engineering and Arts & Science colleges
TNEA — engineering admissions
Scholarships for students
In practice: The free laptop scheme for government college students — this ministry implements it.
🏥
Health & Family Welfare
"From PHCs to super-specialty hospitals"
Why it exists
Healthcare is a public good. The state runs a network of hospitals because private care is unaffordable for most. Doctors, medicines, and medical education need central coordination.
What it controls
Government hospitals — from PHC to AIIMS-level
MBBS and specialist doctor recruitment
Chief Minister's Comprehensive Health Insurance
Vaccination and disease control programs
In practice: Free dialysis and cancer treatment in government hospitals — run through this ministry's scheme.
🍱
Food & Civil Supplies
"Rice on every plate"
Why it exists
Food security is non-negotiable. The Public Distribution System (ration shops) ensures subsidised rice and essentials reach every poor household.
What it controls
Ration shops — over 3.5 crore card holders
Subsidised rice, sugar, kerosene
Price monitoring and consumer protection
Civil Supplies Corporation (procurement)
In practice: Free rice for ration card holders — procured from FCI and distributed via this ministry's network.
🌿 Land & Nature — Agriculture, Forests, Sea
🌾
Agriculture & Farmer Welfare
"Supporting the people who feed the state"
Why it exists
Farming is both a livelihood and a food security issue. Farmers need seeds, credit, crop insurance, and price support — and none of that happens without a dedicated ministry.
What it controls
Subsidised seeds, fertilisers, equipment
Minimum Support Price procurement
Crop insurance and loan waivers
Horticulture — fruits, vegetables, flowers
In practice: A paddy farmer gets free power for his pumpset and subsidised seeds — both from this ministry's schemes.
🌲
Forests
"Protecting what you can't build back"
Why it exists
Tamil Nadu has 17,000+ sq km of forests. Deforestation, poaching, and encroachment are constant threats. A dedicated ministry and IFS officer cadre is the state's response.
What it controls
Reserved forests and wildlife sanctuaries
Anti-poaching enforcement
Human-wildlife conflict management
Green Tamil Nadu reforestation mission
In practice: The elephant corridor between Mudumalai and Anamalai — protected and managed by the Forest Department under this ministry.
🌍
Environment & Climate Change
"Regulating what humans do to nature"
Why it exists
Industrial growth creates pollution. Without regulation, factories dump waste into rivers and air quality degrades. This ministry is the state's environmental watchdog.
What it controls
Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board
Environmental clearances for industries
Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ) enforcement
Climate action plans
In practice: A factory near the Noyyal river in Tiruppur? TNPCB under this ministry monitors its effluent discharge.
🐟
Fisheries & Animal Husbandry
"The sea and the farm animal"
Why it exists
Tamil Nadu has a 1,076 km coastline and millions of fishermen. Fishing communities, livestock farmers, and poultry growers all need state support and regulation.
What it controls
Fisher welfare — boat subsidies, insurance
Aquaculture and inland fishing regulation
Veterinary services — livestock health
Milk and dairy support via AAVIN
In practice: The seasonal fishing ban every April-June — enforced by this ministry to protect fish breeding cycles.
🤝 Welfare — Protecting those who need it most
👩
Social Welfare & Women Empowerment
"Safety nets for women and children"
Why it exists
Women face specific vulnerabilities. Orphaned children, abandoned elders, and abused women can't be left to fend for themselves — this ministry is the state's institutional safety net.
What it controls
₹1000/month Kalaignar Magalir Urimai scheme
Cradle Baby scheme — abandoned newborns
Women's hostels and shelter homes
Anganwadi centres (child nutrition)
In practice: The monthly ₹1000 direct transfer to women in TN — flagship scheme of this ministry.
⚖️
Backward Classes Welfare
"Levelling a historically uneven field"
Why it exists
Caste-based discrimination has historically denied communities access to education and opportunity. This ministry administers reservations and welfare schemes for BC, MBC, and Denotified communities.
What it controls
BC/MBC scholarships and hostel funding
Reservation implementation in education & jobs
Welfare boards for specific communities
In practice: A first-generation college student from a BC community gets a free laptop and hostel — via this ministry's scheme.
🔧
Labour Welfare & Skill Development
"Protecting workers, building careers"
Why it exists
Without labour laws, employers can exploit workers. This ministry enforces minimum wages, ensures safe workplaces, and trains people for jobs.
What it controls
Minimum wage enforcement across industries
Factory inspections and safety audits
ITI (Industrial Training Institutes)
Employment Exchanges
In practice: Textile workers in Tiruppur demanding proper wages — the Labour Department investigates the complaint.
🤲
Minorities Welfare
"Equal opportunity for all faiths"
Why it exists
Religious minorities — Muslims, Christians — often face socioeconomic disadvantages. This ministry ensures access to scholarships, housing, and welfare programs.
What it controls
Wakf Board (Muslim endowments)
Scholarship programs for minority students
NRI Tamil welfare and return support
In practice: A Tamil Muslim student studying in Chennai gets a pre-matric scholarship — via this ministry.
🏆
Youth Welfare & Sports
"From playgrounds to podiums"
Why it exists
Sports infrastructure, training, and talent identification don't happen by accident. A dedicated ministry ensures the state competes nationally and develops youth energy productively.
What it controls
Government stadiums and sports academies
State-level and national games representation
Youth welfare hostels and programmes
Cash awards for medal-winning athletes
In practice: A TN chess or boxing champion gets state funding and training support — via this ministry.
💼 Economy — Trade, Industry & Commerce
🏭
MSME & Industries
"The engine of Tamil Nadu's economy"
Why it exists
Tamil Nadu is India's most industrialised state. Small and medium businesses create most jobs. The ministry attracts investment, sets up industrial estates, and supports entrepreneurs.
What it controls
TIDCO — industrial development corporation
SIPCOT — industrial estates across districts
Investment promotion and MoU signing
Cottage and small industries support
In practice: Global Investors Meet — foreign companies signing MoUs with TN — coordinated by this ministry.
🧵
Handlooms & Textiles
"Protecting an ancient craft industry"
Why it exists
Kanchipuram silk, Salem textiles, and handloom weavers across TN are part of cultural heritage and livelihood. Without protection, power looms and cheap imports would wipe them out.
What it controls
Weaver welfare schemes and subsidies
Co-optex — state handloom cooperative
Textile park development
Yarn price subsidies for weavers
In practice: A Kanchipuram silk weaver sells through Co-optex at a fair price — the ministry's cooperative prevents exploitation.
🤝
Co-operation
"People owning enterprises together"
Why it exists
Cooperatives let farmers, workers, and communities pool resources. Credit societies, milk unions, and consumer stores are all cooperatives — the state registers and regulates them.
What it controls
Primary Agriculture Credit Societies (PACS)
Milk cooperative federations (AAVIN)
Consumer cooperative stores
Cooperative bank regulation
In practice: A farmer getting a crop loan from a village cooperative — that society is registered under this ministry.
🗺️
Tourism
"Making the state worth visiting"
Why it exists
Tourism brings forex and creates jobs. TN's temples, beaches, and hill stations are a huge draw — but they need infrastructure, promotion, and management.
What it controls
TTDC — government hotel and tourism corp
Tourism promotion and branding
Heritage site development
Tourist police
In practice: The "Enchanting Tamil Nadu" tourism campaign — developed and funded by this ministry.
🍶
Prohibition & Excise
"Regulating alcohol — a moral and revenue tension"
Why it exists
Alcohol is both a social problem and a massive revenue source. The state controls its sale, decides where shops can open, collects excise duty, and enforces alcohol laws.
What it controls
TASMAC — state liquor retail monopoly
Liquor pricing and retail licensing
Illicit arrack crackdowns
Excise duty — TN earns ~₹36,000 Cr/year
In practice: TASMAC shops are owned by the state — alcohol revenue alone funds large parts of the state budget.
🎭 Culture & Identity — Language, Faith & Technology
📖
Tamil Development
"Protecting the world's oldest living language"
Why it exists
Tamil is a Classical Language with 2,000+ years of literature. In a globalised world, the state actively promotes and preserves the language — in schools, courts, and public signage.
What it controls
Tamil official language enforcement
Funding for Tamil literature and research
World Tamil Conference organisation
Tamil lexicon and dictionary publishing
In practice: The government mandate to use Tamil in official correspondence — enforced by this ministry.
📰
Information & Publicity
"How the government speaks to the public"
Why it exists
Citizens need to know about government schemes and policies. The state also accredits journalists and manages its own media properties.
What it controls
Doordarshan and All India Radio (state content)
Press accreditation for journalists
Government advertisements in media
Film censor certification (state-level)
In practice: Those scheme advertisement hoardings across Chennai roads — funded and placed by this ministry.
🛕
Hindu Religious & Charitable Endowments (HR&CE)
"Running 38,000 temples"
Why it exists
Tamil Nadu has more than 38,000 Hindu temples, many holding enormous land and wealth. The state administers these institutions to prevent mismanagement and ensure their upkeep and fair access.
What it controls
All major state-controlled temples
Temple trust funds and endowments
Archakar (priest) appointments and salaries
Temple renovation and heritage upkeep
In practice: Tirupati-level temples in TN (like Murugan temples) are administered by HR&CE — their income funds priest salaries and renovation.
💻
Information Technology
"Turning TN into a digital economy"
Why it exists
Chennai is India's second largest tech hub. The state must attract IT companies, build digital infrastructure, and modernise government services — all through a dedicated ministry.
What it controls
TIDEL Park and tech parks across TN
e-Governance — digital citizen services
Cyber security policy for the state
Startup Tamil Nadu ecosystem
In practice: Applying for a birth certificate online via e-Sevai — that digital infrastructure is built by this ministry.
Tamil Nadu Ministry Portfolios — Civics Reference
Based on the structure of the Tamil Nadu Council of Ministers (2021–2026 term, DMK Government).
This cheat sheet covers the purpose and function of each ministry — not the individual office-holders.
Ministries are created by the Chief Minister and can be split, merged, or renamed at any time.
